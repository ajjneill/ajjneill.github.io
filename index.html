About

This is a site for me to organize some thoughts on mathematics (and perhaps a miscellany of other topics), as well as to provide some expositions of topics that fascinate me. I also hope to provide some thoughts on mathematical pedagogy and resources for study. While the site will certainly reflect my current interests, I follow the old Latin proverb docendo discimus, and so it will be largely for me to explore a variety of fairly disconnected topics as I wander through mathematics. I hope the reader engaging in a similar journey will find something of interest!

A remark on organization: Under 'Notes' will be found more lengthy, polished writings, whereas under 'Posts' will be found shorter briefs, some of which will be less polished expository notes, but will include a variety of miscellaneous entries.

About me:

I am a recent graduate of the University of Chicago, where I studied mathematics with excursions into economics, physics, and neuroscience. I am interested in a variety of areas of pure and applied mathematics, ranging from algebraic topology and optimal transport theory to game theory and quantum computation.
